Explanation:

My first observation from the visualization is something we observe using stackoverflow daily as well. Users interact most by opening a question/answer page and surfing to related questions and so on instead of directly searching for all questions under a tag. It also correlates with point 3. wherein I observed that only a small subset of users upvote a lot. This can be explained as them being users of a high reputation who are allowed to upvote, who login to StackOverflow thereby making an effort to increase their stackoverflow presence. They are also the ones who click the most on non-popular tags as they seem to find questions to answer/comment/upvote. My second observation suggests that since the average user navigates using question hyperlinks as his choice of interaction and only popular tags, adaptive systems may need to adapt to this behavour by attaching multiple tags to questions and guiding the user in a more intelligent way through the web application
